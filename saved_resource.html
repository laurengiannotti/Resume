Networks and Information Processing Lab

Software Developer

Software Developer

May 2015 - August 2015
Rochester, NY



I supplemented the computer engineering director's research by adding real-time functionality to a website displaying a visual representation of filtered tweets on a map.

The tweets were displayed as an animation of colored circles appearing at the time a tweet was posted. The tweet's latitude and longitude data was gathered into a MySQL database and queried to determine the placement of the circle on the map. The color of the circle represents the keyword, and the size of the circle indicates the tweets’ popularity based on the number of followers associated with the author's account.





Tweets were displayed on the map only if content within the tweet contained the user-specified keyword. I used Python to collect the tweet attributes, such as the username, keyword, number of retweets and replies, timestamp, and location from which the tweet was sent. The information was stored into a MySQL database.





The information gathered from the project provided insight on how information originates and spreads through social media. I presented the analysis of the results in RIT’s Undergraduate Research Symposium.


This project was a continuation of an already existing Twitter demonstration developed by Biru Cui.

Brookhaven National Laboratory

Research Assistant

Research Assistant

June 2013 - August 2013
Upton, NY

At Brookhaven National Laboratory, I drew conclusions about experimental data on batteries’ discharge behavior in the event of an extended power failure. The purpose for the experiment was to determine if the batteries were sufficiently recharged after a defined number of time constants, or if it was more effective to recharge the batteries for a longer period of time until they reached standard amperage ratings. Due to the fact that the batteries recharge on a non-linear curve, the data analysis revealed that charging can be terminated after three time constants, which significantly reduces recharge time rather than conventionally comparing the batteries’ amperage to a predetermined value.

I gained substantial data management skills from this experience, as the experiment required analysis of over 30 tests each containing thousands of data points. I also developed highly effective communication skills since the analysis required me to quantitatively express my research findings, as well as accurately portray the experiment’s results.

Who we are

The Hot Wheelz Formula SAE Electric is an award-winning performance vehicle team at RIT. We are a multidisciplinary group of engineering students designing and building a fully electric car to race in the annual Formula Hybrid (electric only category) event in Loudon, New Hampshire. We strive to provide a learning environment that exhibits mentorship and teamwork.

Responsibilities

As the 2015 team’s Head of Controller and Programming, I was responsible for leading a sub-group of talented associative designers with all of the programming, wiring, testing, and troubleshooting for the vehicle’s motor controller and CAN communication system. I also coordinated with the team’s rules and safety officer to ensure that the implemented designs meet the requirements for the rigorous technical inspection on race day.

Unique Experiences

In October 2015, I was privileged to participate in an exclusive training event hosted by Curtis Instruments in Mt. Kisco, New York, who generously donated our controller, dashboard, and foot pedal. I, along with seven other team members were exposed to the essentials of programming with Vehicle Control Language (VCL) and integrating the controller into the overall electrical circuit.

Our multidisciplinary team requires versatile skills beyond the scope of a specific engineering discipline. During the summer of 2015, I attended MIG welding classes offered by Mahaney Welding Supply in Rochester, New York. The (50 hour) course consisted of a series of lectures and many opportunities to practice proper techniques. This experience provided our team with the skills required to weld the frame of our car.
